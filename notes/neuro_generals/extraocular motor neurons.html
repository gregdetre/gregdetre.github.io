Extraocular motor neurons

Part of

oculomotor system

Located in

these are nuclei in the brainstem

Function

convert high-level oculomotor signals into position/velocity signals for extraocular muscles

Consists of

abducens nucleus (pons)

oculomotor nucleus (Edinger-Westphal nucleus) (midbrain)

trochlear nucleus (midbrain)

Input from

frontal eye fields

supplementary eye fields

superior colliculus

Send to

extraocular muscles

Mechanism

see oculomotor circuit#mechanism for the inhibitory levels above

components of the signal sent to the extraocular muscles

eye position

eye velocity (from reticular formation)

horizontal eye velocity (pontine reticular formation)

vertical eye velocity (mesencephalic reticular formation)

the discharge frequency of the extraocular motor neurons is directly proportional to the position of the eye and to its velocity

pulse from burst cells in reticular formation

height of pulse of activity = velocity

height of step = amplitude of saccade

eye held fixed by inhibitory omnipause neurons in the reticular formation

difference between initial and final discharges = step

damage

ophthalmoparesis
