cAMP

signal molecule (e.g. epinephrine) binds to cell surface receptor protein

activates a G protein on the inside of the cell

the G protein stimulates adenylyl cyclase to produce large amount of cyclic AMP within the cell (using up ATP)

the cAMP then binds to + activates a target protein (e.g. alpha-kinase)

adds phosphates to specific proteins in the cell

the effect of this depends on the identify of the cell and the proteins being phosphorylated

Ca++ as second messenger

signal molecule binds to cell surface receptor protein

activates a G protein

this activates enzyme, phospholiphase C, which catalyzes production of inositol triphosphate

which diffuses through the cytoplasm and binds to endoplasmic reticulum, opening the calcium channels

the calcium ions flow out of the endoplasmic reticulum into the cytoplasm

and trigger proteins to initiate a variety of activities

Wikipedia on second messenger

second messengers = low-weight diffusible molecules

used in signal transduction to relay signals within a cell

synthesized and released by specific enzymatic reactions

as a result of an external signal received by a transmembrane receptor

pre-processed by other membrane-associated proteins

three basic types of second messenger molecules

  • hydrophobic

    ...

  • hydrophilic

    water-soluble molecules

    e.g. cAMP, cGMP and Ca++

    located within the cytosol

  • gases, nitric oxide, carbon monoxide

    usually trigger a cyclic GMP cascade

all these intracellular messengers:

can be synthesized/released and broken down again in specific reactions by enzymes

some (like Ca++) can be stored in special organelles and released quickly when needed

production/release and destruction can be localized, enabling the cell to limit space and time of signal activity

Wikipedia on second messenger system

second messenger system = method of cellular signalling where the signalling molecule doesn't entire the cell

utilizes a cascade of events that transduces the signal into a cellular change

utilize receptors on the surface of the plasma membrane

generally coupled to a kinase on the interior surface of the membrane

the kinase phosphorylates another molecule (often cAMP) which carries out a further action

there's another common second messenger system involving calcium
