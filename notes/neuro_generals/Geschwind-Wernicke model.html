Part of

aphasia

Mechanism

Wernickes area for processing acoustic images of words

sends to Brocas area for articulation of speech

via arcuate fasciculus

both Brocas area and Wernickes area interact with polymodal association cortex

nonverbal meaning: converted into acoustic images in Wernickes area and turned into vocalizations by Brocas area

reading: visual input from left visual cortices

writing: motor output from Exners area

Geschwind claimed to have found patients with conduction aphasia - not really clear though

predicted

problems producing spontaneous speech and repeating speech and incorrect use of words

can understand words that they hear/see and can hear their own speech errors, but cannot repair them

Issues

now realize that arcuate fasciculus is bidirectional

apparently, you also get repetition impairment with Wernickes aphasia (i.e. damage to Wernickes area)

also, double dissociation for nouns and verbs

doesn't fit with the idea that Brocas aphasia patients have trouble with grammatically complicated sentences
