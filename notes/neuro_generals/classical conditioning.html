Classical conditioning

Aka

Pavlovian conditioning

Part of

associative learning

See also

operant conditioning

fear conditioning

pavlov

Definition

learning a relationship between two stimuli

i.e.

operant conditioning = behavior -> reward

classical conditioning = stimulus -> stimulus

Function

conditioned stimulus (CS)

e.eg. light, tone, tactile stimulus

produces no/little overt response

unconditioned stimulus (US) = reinforcement

e.g. food, shock

unconditioned response

strong, consistent, overt response to the unconditioned stimulus - produced without learning

e.g. salivation, withdrawal

condition response

when a CS is followed by a US, the CS will begin to elicit a new/different response

in other words, the CS becomes predictive of the US

extinction = reversing this

not just contiguity or the number of times they're paired

it's more like correlation

Mechanisms

thompson - protective eyeblink reflex in rabbits

CS = tone

US = puff of air

CR = eyeblink response

lesion to the vermis or the interpositus nucleus (both in cerebellum) abolishes CR
