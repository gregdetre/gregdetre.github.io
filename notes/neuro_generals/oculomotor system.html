Definition

oculomotor system = moves the eyes in the orbit

simplest motor system - 2 sets of 6 extraocular muscles

History

Landott (1890) - noticed jerky movements (saccades) as we read

Dodge (1902) outlined 5 separate movement systems to put the fovea on a target and keep it there

same effector pathway - three bilateral gropus of oculomotor neurons in the brainstem

see #mechanisms

Function

to bring targets onto the fovea

to keep them there

Mechanisms

cortex chooses target

high-level cortical signals (frontal eye fields and supplementary eye fields)

do not specify the contribution of each of the extraocular muscles

relayed to the extraocular motor neurons in the brainstem by the superior colliculus

these extraocular motor neurons control the individual contributions of the extraocular muscles at a low level

the tonic inhibition of the superior colliculus by the substantia nigra pars reticulata is suppressed by the caudate nucleus

Clinical

diplopia

can diagnoze most oculomotor deficits behaviorally
