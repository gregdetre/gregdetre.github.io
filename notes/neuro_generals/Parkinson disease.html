Parkinson disease

Definition

impaired movement (hypokinetic)

also disturbances of affect, behavior and cognition

See also

motor disorders

Huntington disease

basal ganglia

dopamine

Causation

caused by loss of dopaminergic neurons in the pars compacta of substantia nigra

acetylcholine too high, and dopamine too low in the striatum

what does the acetylcholine in the striatum do???

direct pathway activity is reduced, and indirect pathway activity is increased

i.e. the indirect pathway is stronger than the direct pathway

so the thalamus is inhibited much more

loss of dopamine in the putamen is greatest on side contralateral to the more affected side

this might be why you get involuntary saccades to the contralateral side (see oculomotor circuit)!

Clinical

Symptoms

akinesia and bradykinesia (see hypokinetic) are both seen in Parkinson's disease

difficulty initiating movements & stopping them once started

tremor (at rest)

rigidity (hypertonia in flexors & extensors)

loss of "associated movements" (e.g., swinging arms while walking)

Parkinson disease patients get hallucinations and tics (e.g. De Niro in Awakenings)

L-DOPA treatment

see L-DOPA

dopamine cannot cross blood-brain barrier

use L-DOPA (synthetic precursor to dopamine) instead

can cross the blood-brain barrier and be converted to dopamine

benefits begin to wane after about 5 years

over-treatment can induce choreiform ('dance-like') movements (see hyperkinetic)

MPTP

William Langston discovered that drug addicts exposed to MPTP develop a profound Parkinsonian state

besides the increased tonic output of the internal globus pallidus, MPTP monkeys also show changes in the pattern of phasic discharge

synchronized oscillatory discharge from the basal ganglia may be causing the tremors

Lesioning

excessive activity in the indirect pathway at the subthalamic nucleus seems to be a big factor in Parkinson disease signs

lesioning the sensorimotor bit of the subthalamic nucleus or the internal globus pallidus seems to really improve MPTP-treated monkeys

so pallidotomy is sometimes used for helping medically intractable cases of Parkinson disease

apparently Michael J Fox had a pallidotomy

Simulations

simulated as: low levels of dopamine in the pars compacta

diminished tonic dopamine, and diminished (positive) phasic dopamine bursts

favours the no-go pathway - fits with Parkinson's patients' troubles initiating movements

so Frank predicted they'd have problems learning from positive reinforcement

however, they can still show the phasic dopamine decreases

though perhaps with a slightly more limited range

so their capacity for learning from negative reinforcement should be relatively spared

interesting control group - Parkinson's patients taking L-DOPA

should have more dopamine in their system than normal controls

should have more tonic dopamine, and so diminished capacity for phasic dopamine dips

should have problems learning from negative reinforcement

but should have phasic dopamine increases - spared positive reinforcement learning

Frank, ... & O'Reilly (Science, 2005)

two ways to solve the AB discrimination

  • associate A with go
  • associate B with no-go

train on A vs B discrimination

test A vs C, D, E, F

compute the average probability of choosing A

if A is strongly associated with go, then subjects will show a strong tendency to choose A

test B vs C, D, E, F

if B is strongly associated with no-go, then subjects will show a strong tendency to choose against B

so Parkinson disease patients will rely primarily on no-go learning

  • normal tendency to choose against B
  • impaired tendency to choose A

and vice versa for the L-DOPA patients

low tonic dopamine - operationalized by lower tonic input to pars compacta units

parameters are out of the range that the system was designed for

you start to see regular oscillations between external globus pallidus and subthalamic nucleus, as a result of all the feedback loops

could be a neural substrate of tremors

is the no-go pathway necessary?

it greatly enhances performance in the model

it's useful to have the ability to specifically say 'stimulus X should not trigger response Y'

lots of complex machinery - aimed at instilling a strong negative bias on initiating actions
