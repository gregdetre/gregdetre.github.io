Hubel & Wiesel

Huber & Wiesel (1959) identified three types of cells:

- simple cells

orientation-tuned

separate excitatory and inhibitory regions

- complex cells

orientation-tuned

often directional-selective

no separate excitatory or inhibitory regions

- hyper-complex cells

orientation-tuned

often directional-selective

no separate excitatory or inhibitory regions

selective for length (either one or both ends)

Lettvin et al (1959) first provided evidence of a frog's 'bug detector', certain ganglion cells in a frog's optic nerve which respond intensely to a small, dark object which moves around within a certain retinal region.
