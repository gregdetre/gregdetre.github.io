ophthalmoparesis

Function

the eye is moved by three complementary pairs of extraocular muscles:

  • complements within eyes

    medial and lateral horizontal recti

  • complements across eyes

    superior oblique and inferior rectus

    inferior oblique and superior rectus

controlled by three cranial nerves

lateral rectus

innervated by abducens nerve

superior oblique

innervated by trochlear nerve

medial, inferior and superior recti and inferior oblique innervated by the oculomotor nerve

damage

ophthalmoparesis

superior oblique

hooked around the trochlear (pulley-like structure)

whose primary action is intorsion and whose secondary actions are to abduct (laterally rotate) and depress the eyeball (i.e. it makes the eye move outward and downward)
