Hormone

secreted by an endocrine gland

transported to target tissue

bind to receptor sites

chemical messenger from one cell/group to another

hormones are produced by all multicellular organisms

mainly produced by endocrine glands

but also by nearly every organ system and tissue type in the body

functions, e.g.

stimulation or inhibition growth

induction or suppression of apoptosis (programmed cell death)

activation/inhibition of immune system

regulating metabolism

preparation for new activity or phase of life

rate of hormone production is often regulated by negative feedback - homeostasis

four types of vertebrate hormones

amine-derived hormones

catecholamines

epinephrine

dopamine

norepinephrine

tryptophan derivatives

melatonin

serotonin

tyrosine derivatives

peptide hormones

chains of amino acids

e.g. TRH, vasopressin, adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH), corticotropin, corticotropin releasing hormone (CRH)

peptides composed of scores or hundreds of amino acids are referred to as proteins

protein hormones

e.g. insulin, growth hormones

steroid hormones

adrenal cortex = one of the primary sources

e.g. testosterone, glucocorticoids (cortisol)

lipid and phospholipid hormones

Etymology

Gk, 'to set in motion'
