Melotonin

produced in the pineal gland

serotonin is a precursor to melotonin

may be involved in sleep

in light we produce much less melotonin

melotonin shifts/resets the sleep-wake cycle

melotonin causes sleep but it doesn't necessarily have to be there to fall asleep (e.g. during the day)

some indication that melatonin levels are lower in elderly insomniacs relative to age matched non-insomniacs, and melatonin therapy in such cases appears modestly beneficial in correcting the problem

from web - pineal gland and melotonin

melotonin therapy used for jetlag and shift workers - doesn't seem too helpful

may be involved in reproductive function in some species

inhibits the secretion of the gonadotropic hormones from the anterior pituitary gland

other effects:

decrease motor activity

induce fatigue

lower body temperature

(may be part of the reason for the effect on sleep-wake cycles)
