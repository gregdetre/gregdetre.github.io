Production aphasia

Aka

production aphasia

non-fluent aphasia

expressive aphasia

grammatic aphasia

Part of

aphasia

Located in

Brocas area

for it to persist, also have to be lesions in

surrounding frontal fields (external aspect of Brodmann area 6, 8, 9, 10 and 46)

underlying white matter

insula

basal ganglia

small portion of the anterior superior temporal gyrus

see anterior insula

Definition

ability to understand language but impaired ability to speak coherently

see example dialog

like transcortical motor aphasia, but repetition is impaired

Unimpaired

originally thought that comprehension was unimpaired

however, see below for discussion of grammatically complex sentence comprehension

but still able to communicate verbally, because the selection of words, especially nouns, is usually correct

not so good at verbs or grammatical words

speech retains some grammatical structure, e.g. subject-verb-object phrase order

can make some fine grammatical discriminations

Impaired

disrupted syntax

hesitations and stumbles

consists mainly of content words

rather like telegraphic speech (the two-word stage of language development that children go through, producing phrases like 'sleep blanket')

effortful

lacks melodic intonation of normal speech

over-learned and rote-learned speech patterns may be retained (e.g. counting from 1-10)

but cannot produce the same numbers in conversation

patients who recover say that they knew what they wanted to say but could not express themselves

often residual deficits

trouble comprehending grammatically complex sentences

unable to repeat complex sentences

apparently, unable to comprehend sentences whose meaning can't be pieced together from the indivdual meanings of content words + semantic knowledge

e.g. 'the boy that the girl is chasing is tall'

Mechanism

Geschwind-Wernicke model explains as damage to Brocas area (e.g. patient Tan)

see Geschwind-Wernicke model#issues

see Brocas area#mechanism

Example dialog

(from Gardner, 1975)

E: Were you in the Coast Guard?

P: No, er, yes, yes...ship...Massachu...chusetts..Coast Guard...years. [raises hands twice with fingers indicating 19]

E: Oh, you were in the Coast Guard for 19 years.

P: Oh...boy...right...right.

E: Why are you in the hospital?

P: [Points to paralyzed arm] Arm no good. [Points to mouth] Speech...can't say...talk, you see.

E: What happened to make you lose speech?

P: Head, fall, Jesus Christ, me no good, str, str...oh Jesus...stroke.

E: Could you tell me what you've been doing in the hospital?

P: Yes, sure. Me go, er, uh, P. T. nine o'cot, speech...two times...read...wr...ripe, er, rike, er, write...practice...get-ting better.
