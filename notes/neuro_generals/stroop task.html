Stroop task

Part of

cognitive control

Definition

Cohen, Dunbar & McClelland, 1990 - model

attend to one dimension and ignore competing prepotent dimension

top-down input from the task-demand units pushes the input (word and color units) into the middle, steepest part of their sigmoidal activation function

attention - guide the flow of activity along one pathway while attenuating the flow along another

= 'guided activation theory of cognitive control (Miller & Cohen, 2001)

in terms of controlled vs automatic processing

color naming = controlled

without attention to color, subjects will read the word

has no impact on word reading, even when it conflicts with the word being read

word reading = automatic

even when asked to name the color, the presence of a conflicting word will slow the response

= the classic Stroop effect (MacLeod, 1991; Stroop, 1935)

because the word s processed even without the allocation of attention

that is, if you buy into the dichotomous controlled vs automatic story
