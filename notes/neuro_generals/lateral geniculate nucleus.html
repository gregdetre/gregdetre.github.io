Lateral geniculate nucleus

Definition

layered - 'geniculate' = 'bent like a knee'

Located in

thalamus (???)

Consists of

6 layers:

2, 3, 5 = ipsilateral

1, 4, 6 = contralateral

1, 2 = magnocellular

3, 4, 5 and 6 = parvocellular

koniocellular (or 'interlaminar')

between each of the M and P layers

zone of very small cells

these 3 types of cells correspond with similarly-named retinal ganglion cells

Mechanism

see parallel visual pathways#integration for information on selectively blocking M and P pathways

Misc

LGN similar organisation to retinal ganglion cells

wavelength sensitive antagonistic surrounds Y/B, R/G

response to light change sby activity in the rest of the brain

'gating' - common to other thalamic relays

the various layers are strictly in register, insofar as:

same area of retina -> single radial column

though: distortion - center higher representation

optic radiation -> occipital lobe

off-center-on-surround plus opposite in LGN

comes from 2 retinal bipolar cells, one to each

both looking for edges, but opposite sides

you need that duplication because otherwise they couldn't signal local reductions if they're already firing at low frequency, hence their complements
