Mechanism

selective processing results from

competition amongst relevant stimuli

top-down biases to determine the outcome of this competition

(Moran & Desimone, 1985)

larger changes in firing when there are multiple sitmuli in the RF and one must be attended

compared to just the attended stimulus with no competition

i think they mean that the effect of attention is greater when there are multiple stimuli

which would be consistent with the idea that you get overall diminished response when there are multiple stimuli in the same RF

claims:

neurons that respond to a stimulus will be suppressed when a second stimulus is added that activates a competiting population of neurons

and that attention will resolve the competition in favour of the preferred stimulus

i.e. biased by spatially-specific feedback signals

and on dorsal stream (Recanzone & Wurtz, 2000)
