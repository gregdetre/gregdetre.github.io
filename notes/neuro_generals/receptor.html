Receptors

there are parts of the retina where glutatmate is inhibitory, and other parts where GABA is excitatory - depends on the receptor and postsynaptic mechanisms

all receptors for neurotransmitters have two biochemical features in common

  • membrane-spanning proteins
  • typically influence the opening or closing of ion channels

two ways in which postsynaptic receptors can gate ion channels:

  • directly using ionotropic receptors

    rapid behaviors, e.g. ACh at neuromuscular junction

  • indirectly using metabotropic receptors

    slower but longer-lasting effects, useful for modulating behavior

    e.g. serotonin, norepinephrine

action of the neurotransmitter in the postsynaptic cell depends on the receptors that recognize and bind the transmitter

not the transmitter itself
