Mechanism

stimulation -> bilateral movements

like the lateral premotor cortex, the medial premotor cortex plays a role in the selection of movement, but

the lateral premotor cortex makes the greater contribution when the subject uses external cues to direct the movements, and the

medial premotor cortex when no such cues are available

evidence for this

lesions

monkeys with medial premotor cortex lesions

severly impaired at self-paced arm movements

relearning motor sequences

only slightly impaired at making arm movements cued by an external signal

monkeys with lateral premotor cortex lesions

impaired at relearning a visual conditional motor task

from PET scanning

medial premotor cortex is activated when subjects perform a well-rehearsed sequence from memory

but there is more activation in the lateral premotor cortex when subjects must rely on external feedback to learn new sequences

single unit recording

more cells active in the medial premotor cortex

when repetitive movements are self-paced

when the animals perform motor sequences from memory

more cells active in the lateral premotor cortex

when these movements are externally triggered

when they learn new sequences as directed by visual cues
