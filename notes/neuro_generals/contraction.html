Contraction

100+ motor neurons leave motor nucleus inside spinal cord via ventral root

each neuron makes contact and then branches out, making contact with 100-1000 muscle fibers

so a single action potential can activate hundreds of muscle fibers in synchrony

detectable with electromyogram (EMG)

each synapse (neuromuscular junction) onto a muscle (= end-plate region) contains many acetylcholine vesicles

motor neurons transmit enough acetylcoline to the muscle to depolarize the postsynaptic membrane

activates voltage-gated calcium ion channels, so calcium rushes in

causes an action potential to propagate along the sarcolemma (= membrane of the muscle fiber)

slowly, in both directions away from the end-plate region

depolarizes the sarcoplasmic reticulum, causing calcium release

calcium binds to troponin in the thin fibrils

exposes actin-binding site to myosin

myosin and actin form cross bridge

lots of action potentials in a row -> more cross bridges

all the fibers that contact a neuron respond synchronously

the currents sum and cause muscle contraction
