Hypothalamus

Part of

part of hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis - stress response

Consists of

groups of specialized nuclei. divided into 3 regions

anterior hypothalamus

medial hypothalamus

posterior hypothalamus

also (not sure where these fit in)

lateral hypothalamus

ventromedial hypothalamus

arcuate nucleus

Located in

below the thalamus

major portion of ventral region of diencephalon

Input from

A1/A2 cell groups in medulla

sensory information

Sends to

hypothalamus controls endocrine system

  1. directly through secretion of neuroendocrine products into the general circulation from the posterior pituitary gland
  2. indirectly by secreting regulatory hormones into the local blood vessels of the anterior pituitary gland

Function

regulate metabolic and autonomic processes

links nervous system to endocrine system via pituitary gland

compares sensory information against biological set points, restores homeostasis

controls 5 basic homeostatic requirements

  • blood pressure and electrolyte composition

    drinking and salt appetite

    blood osmolality and vasomotor tone

  • regulates body temperature
  • energy metabolism

    regulates feeding, digestion and metabolic rate

  • regulates reproduction

    hormonal control of mating, pregnancy and lactation

  • emergency responses to stress

    secretion of adrenal stress hormones

also circadian rhythm (suprachiasmatic nucleus)

pain relay

also motivation

regulates consummatory behavior

Mechanism

heating the hypothalamus induces sleep in animals

from Cannon-Bard theory of emotion - studied cats with sham rage

coordinated rage response did not occur if posterior hypothalamus is transected from the rest of brainstem

Etymology

from Greek = 'under the thalamus'
