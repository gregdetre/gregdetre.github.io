Priming

form of implicit memory

even small cortical weight changes can yield one-trial learning effects

cortex appears to be the key substrate for these priming effects

even patients with focal damage to the hippocampus show this effect

though could perhaps be BG

weight-based, since the effects are long-lasting

perceptual identification priming lasts weeks or months after just a brief exposure to a stimulus

Stem completion priming

win___ -> 'window'

then much later, they'll be more likely to complete win___ -> 'window'

or you could show them 'handle, winter, shower', and they'll be more likely to complete to 'winter'

Phonetic priming

or, if you ask people to spell 'reed' (out loud), they'll say 'read' unless you first ask them to name a musical instrument that uses a reed

Perceptual identification priming and category generation priming

show 'kiwi', then ask them to name some fruits

Negative priming

two visual stimuli, where you have to ignore one. you'll then be slower to recognise it later
