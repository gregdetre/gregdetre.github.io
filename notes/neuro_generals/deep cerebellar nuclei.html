Deep cerebellar nuclei

Located in

center of cerebellum

embedded in white matter

Consists of

3 pairs of deep cerebellar nuclei:

  • fastigial nucleus
  • interposed nucleus
    • globose nucleus
    • emboliform nucleus
  • dentate nucleus (lateral cerebellar hemispheres)

mnemonic (from lateral to medial) = 'Don't eat greasy food'

  • fastigial nucleus (vermis)
  • interposed nucleus (in paravermis/intermediate zone)
  • dentate nucleus (lateral cerebellar hemispheres)

Input from

topographic inhibitory (GABA-ergic) inputs from Purkinje cells in Purkinje layer of cerebellar cortex

excitatory (glutamate) inputs from cerebellar mossy fibers

Send to

premotor cortex

most cerebellar outputs come from the deep cerebellar nuclei

except outputs from vestibulocerebellum go directly to vestibular nuclei

Function

regulate the gain and timing of motor actions

Mechanism

tonically active

powerful excitatory potentials
