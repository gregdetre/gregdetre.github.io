Muscle force

contractile force depends on

level of activation of each muscle fiber

length of each muscle fiber

velocity of each muscle fiber

large fibers attached to fast-twitch fibers

enhances ability to produce large forces rapidly

large cell bodies, large diameter axons, conduct action potentials faster

motor units recruited in fixed order from weakest to strongest

  • slow twitch
  • fast twitch fatigue-resistant
  • fast twitch fatiguable

active tension in a muscle varies with the rate of stimulation of the muscle nerve

increasing rate of stimulation produces higher mean force

overall force of a contraction depends on

number of active motor units

size of active motor units

firing rates of active motor units

active force produced by muscle depends on

number of action potentials

fiber length (myosin/actin overlap)

number of cross bridges formed

depends on degree of overlap between actin and myosin filaments

when stretched out, less active force

as the sarcomeres shorten, actin filaments from each end of the sarcomere overlap with a longer portion of thick filaments -> greater contractile force

so greatest contractile force = when sarcomere is in the middle (not too short, not too long)
