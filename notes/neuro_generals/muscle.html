muscle force

muscle groups

Consists of

each muscle contains many muscle fibers

each muscle fiber contains many myofibrils

each of the myofibrils contains many sarcomeres

each sarcomere contains thick filaments and thin filaments

Mechanism

each muscle is controlled by multiple (how many???) motor neurons

3 types of muscle

  • skeletal muscle
  • smooth muscle
  • cardiac muscle

most muscle types use the movement of actin against myosin to create contraction and relaxation (sliding filament model)

muscle force depends on many factors

derived from mesodermal layer of embryonic germ cells

accounts for most of the body's energy consumption

store energy in glycogen (= 1% of muscle mass)

can be rapidly converted to glucose

Etymology

latin, 'little mouse', e.g. biceps pops up as though a mouse were scurrying under the skin
