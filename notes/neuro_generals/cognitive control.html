Adaptive updating & gating

update the representations appropriately

resist perturbation by irrelevant inputs (avoid distraction)

but respond to inputs that signal the need for change (avoid perseveration)

dopamine-mediated adaptive gating mechanism subserved by ventral tegmental area

simple transient gating signal in PFC units (Braver & Cohen, 2000)

made the PFC units temporarily responsive to input from posterior structures

without the gating signals, the PFC units ignored outside input

i.e. maintained in the face of interference

new complex dopaminergic model (Frank et al, 2001)

more focused forms of gating

hierarchical updating of goals

Limited capacity, conflict and the locus ceruleus

why limited capacity?

the role of attention is to reduce conflicts in processing

appearance of conflict provides a signal that attentional control is needed

anterior cingulate cortex monitors for conflict, and alerts the prefrontal cortex

but the prefrontal cortex allocates the control

this contrasts with the details of the Posner & Petersen (1990) anterior attention control system

model the effects of norepinephrine release as a change in the gain (steepness) of PFC neurons' activation function

i.e. locus ceruleus binarizes activity and enhances contrast in the PFC, based on ACC activity

in this way, the ACC can augment control without specific knowledge about which particular representations in PFC require augmentation

i.e. a global signal (norepinephrine) can have specific effects

N.B. phasic (transient) (rather than sustained/tonic) release of norepinephrine modulates the PFC
