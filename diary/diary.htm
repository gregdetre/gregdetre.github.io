My first few days at Harvard

Saturday 7th Sep 2002

Had a brilliant day - played the most international game of football ever. This is a photo of the two Brazilian guys I’ve spent lots of time with so far. That’s the statue of John Harvard - all the tourists take photos of this while touching his foot. The undergraduates know this, so every time they get drunk, they take turns to piss all over the base of the statue. It’s not quite as much fun as pissing on tourists, but almost. Miguel and Marcelo know this story, so if you look very carefully, their hands are hovering a couple of millimetres above.

Sunday 8th Sep 2002

sorry i haven’t written more often, but it has been a truly truly crazy few days, and i’m very much hoping that things show no signs of calming down. have been to a few bars, spoken at least a little to pretty much everyone on my corridor, had a wander round, blah blah blah. These photos were all taken with Marcelo’s camera, though I wasn’t actually even there. These aren’t my friends. I’ve broken into his room and stolen them from his hard disk. The reality is that no one here will talk to me at all.

Monday 9th Sep 2002

There were cheerleaders and I missed it. I am very unhappy about this, and assure you it won’t happen again.

Tuesday 10th Sep 2002

the big surprise was that although harvard courses don’t start for another few days, MIT courses started the day i arrived. oops. so i’ve spent today and yesterday charging from one class to another trying to figure out which are best. it looks as though i’m going to sign up with a crazy man who invented the little round pimple mice on laptops. that’s pretty fucking cool, as far as i’m concerned.

Wednesday 11th Sep 2002

the MIT media lab is where geeks end up when they die and go to heaven. the first time i crawled into its air-conditioned multi-colour high-tech sanctity from the hot-as-hell, polluted, people-filled bedlam outside, i genuinely nearly wet myself with excitement. there are massive flatscreen monitors with cool colours and casual people wandering around talking about interesting stuff and wires and lights everywhere. however, classes have been a bit mixed in quality so far, so i’ve signed up to the one i mentioned above and i’m going to sit in on a couple of others. it turns out that the rule that even supposedly v bright students can be inane holds true globally.

Thursday 12th Sep 2002

i went to see the guy at harvard who does cool things with computers and language, and had yet another of those alarming discussions where we both realised that i know nothing relevant, but where i do my level best to hide it. this is becoming pretty habitual for me now, and, to be honest, i’m thinking of making it a policy not to learn about anything i do know anything whatsoever about. this seems to me to be either true wisdom or total folly. i’ll know which for sure in a year’s time. anyway, so i think i’ve convinced him to let me take his course, but i’ve also promised to take at least 2 other hardcore computer science courses in parallel and read a textbook. i am marginally daunted.
