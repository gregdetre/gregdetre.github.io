Vestibular nystagmus

oculomotor system

nystagmus

Definition

vestibular nystagmus = when the eyes flick backwards (quick phase) during sustained rotation

Function

in the dark, the semicircular canals adapt after a few seconds (although brain stem circuitry extends this habituation time)

so during sustained or slow head movement the vestibular signal ultimately fails and the eyes begin to move in space

however, in the light, the opto-kinetic system compensates for the defects in the vestibular system by using the visual motion of head movement to drive the eyes

vestibular nystagmus is always horizontal
