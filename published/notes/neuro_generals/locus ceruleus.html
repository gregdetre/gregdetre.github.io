  • tonic (spontaneous discharge)

    greater during waking than sleep/drowsy

    two levels (statistically robust difference of 1-2 spikes per second)

    • intermediate

      good task performance

    • elevated

      monkeys were distractible and unable to focus on the task

      may promote quick physical reactions in dangerous/unpredictable situations

  • phasic

    in fn2, they divide phasic responses into stimulus-evoked responses and responses related to physical orienting toward a given stimulus

    fire for behaviorally relevant stimuli, i.e. target stimulus

    preceded behavioral response by 200ms

    their latencies were correlated with the animal's response latencies

    differentiated between relevant/non-relevant stimuli best during periods of intermediate spontaneous activity

    responds most for physical orienting towards stimuli

    most intensie when ongoing behavior (sleep, grooming, feeding) is interrupted and the animal orients (Aston-Jones and Blum, 1981)
