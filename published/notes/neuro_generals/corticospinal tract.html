Corticospinal tract

Aka

pyramidal tract

Consists of

lateral corticospinal tract

medial corticospinal tract

Inputs from

1/3 from primary motor cortex (Betz cells)

1/3 from premotor cortex

1/3 from somatosensory cortex

Sends to

ventral horn of spinal cord

Function

control most of the body - trunk and limb muscles, but not face

Mechanism

massive collection of motor axons from cortex -> spinal cord

direct corticospinal tract: neurons from motor cortex synapse onto spinal cord nuclei

indirect corticobulbar tract: neurons from motor cortex synapse onto brainstem nuclei, then onto spinal cord

corticospinal tract and corticobulbar tract run together till the midbrain

they separate in the pons

they regroup in the medulla to form the medullary pyramid.

then corticospinal tract splits at pyramidal decussation into

lateral corticospinal tract

ventral corticospinal tract

both lateral corticospinal tract and ventral corticospinal tract terminate in approximately the same regions of spinal gray matter as do the lateral brainstem pathway and medial brainstem pathway

so control of axial muscles and distal muscles is separated from each other, but info from motor cortex and brainstem for different muscles are kept together

Two things are always kept separate

muscles from face/head run in separate tract (corticobulbar tract)

muscles that are important for posture, etc - run in a separate tract (ventral corticospinal tract)

Major input to motor cortex: prefrontal, parietal, temporal association areas. Also from BG and cerebellum.
