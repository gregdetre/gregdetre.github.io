Pathway

overview

primary afferents ascend to medulla ipsilaterally in dorsal columns of spinal cord

secondary afferents cross in the medulla and ascend as the medial lemniscus

in the thalamus they synapse in the ventroposterior lateral nucleus and finally ascend to primary somatosensory cortex

information from periphery enters through sensory neurons sitting outside the spinal cord in a clump called a dorsal root ganglion

one dorsal root ganglion for every spinal nerve

signal passes directly from the distal axon process to the proximal process, bypassing the cell body

= the primary afferents

ascend in the dorsal white matter of the spinal cord

= dorsal columns aka posterior columns

divided into

gracile fasciculus

cuneate fasciculus

at the medulla, the primary afferents connect to the secondary afferents

gracile fasciculus connects to the gracile nucleus

cuneate fasciculus connects to the cuneate nucleus

secondary afferents leave the gracile nucleus and the cuneate nucleus

line up in the ventral medulla

and cross immediately to the contralateral brainstem

form a new tract called the medial lemniscus

ascend all the way through the brainstem to the thalamus

in the thalamus, the secondary afferents in the medial lemniscus connect to a third set of afferents

in a thalamic nucleus called the ventrolateral posterior nucleus (VPL)

then these thalamocortical afferents

travel up through the internal capsule

to the primary somatosensory cortex

located in the post-central gyrus

= the fold of cortex just posterior to the central sulcus
