Anterior pituitary gland

Located in

pituitary gland

Mechanism

when the periventricular hypothalamus releases corticotropin releasing hormone into the bloodstream nearby

anterior pituitary gland releases adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH)

this then stimulates the release of cortisol from the adrenal gland

the arcuate nucleus produces dopamine that inhibits prolactin production in the anterior pituitary gland

prolactin production is normally increased during stress

no known specific role though

indirectly regulated by hypothalamus

releasing hormones and release-inhibiting hormones are released by hypothalamus and travel via blood supply to anterior pituitary gland

anterior pituitary gland then releases its own hormones in response

affects adrenal cortex, thyroid, gonads, growth hormones and prolactin (see ks4 980)
