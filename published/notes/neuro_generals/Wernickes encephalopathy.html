Wernickes encephalopathy

See also

Korsakoffs syndrome

Definition

Mechanism

damage to mammillary bodies cranial nerve nuclei III, IV, VI, and VIII

also thalamus, hypothalamus, periaqueductal grey, vermis and dorsal nucleus of vagus nerve

thiamine issues (see Korsakoffs syndrome)

ataxia & ophthalmoparesis

from lesions in oculomotor (III, IV and VI) and vestibular (VIIIth) cranial nerves

symptoms

loss of short-term memory

acute onset

nystagmus and gaze palsies

ophthalmoplegia

gait ataxia

confusion

not related to Wernickes aphasia

treatment

thiamine (intravenous or intramuscular)

rehydration
