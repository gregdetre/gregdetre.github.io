Associative visual agnosia

Part of

visual agnosia

Unimpaired

can faithfully reproduce detailed drawings

can identify objects

by selecting the correct drwaing

Impaired

cannot name seen objects but can identify objects

Narrow associative visual object agnosics

cannot name or even indicate recognition of any visually-presented object, verbally or non-verbally

even though their naming of tactile and auditory stimuli is relatively unimpaired

despite evidence from detailed drawing (though done slowly and line-by-line) that their basic perceptual abilities are up to the task

Further details

seems kind of like optic aphasia

in that they have difficulty naming seen objects but can identify them

Mechanism

damage to posterior parietal cortex
