GABA

inhibitory neurotransmitter - gamma-aminobutyric acid

binds to and activates GABA receptors on dendrite

open channels that allow chlorine (Cl-) ions in

inhibitory postsynaptic potentials = produce an inhibitory effect on the postsynaptic neuron

two subtypes of GABA receptors

GABA-A

GABA-B - stays open longer, and so has a more protracted inhibitory effect

most common inhibitory neurotransmitter in cortex

lots of nice chemicals that affect the GABA receptors

muscamol - more powerful and long-lasting inhibition than GABA

bicuculline - stops GABA from getting in, and so reduces inhibitory inputs

apparently there are parts where GABA is excitatory - see receptors - depends on receptor/polysynaptic mechanisms
