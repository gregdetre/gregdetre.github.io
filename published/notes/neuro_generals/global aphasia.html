Global aphasia

Part of

aphasia

Definition

combination of Brocas aphasia, Wernickes aphasia, and conduction aphasia

Unimpaired

nondeliberate ('automatic') speech, e.g. expletives

routines, e.g. coutning

singing previously-learned songs

auditory limited to a few owrds and idiomatic expressions

Impaired

all comprehension, production, repetition

may be able to repeat the same word etc.

weakness in teh right side fo the face, paralysis of the right limbs

Mechanism

damage to

anterior langauage region, basal ganglia, insula (as in Brocas aphasia)

superior temporal gyrus (as in conduction aphasia)

posterior langauge regions (Wernickes aphasia)

usually caused by large infarct in the region supplied by the middle cerebral artery
