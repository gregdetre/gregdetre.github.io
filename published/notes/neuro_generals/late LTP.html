Late LTP

Aka

late protein synthesis-dependent phase

L-LTP

Details

lasts from days to months

pronounced strengthening of postsynaptic response

involves the synthesis of new proteins

Possible causes

glutamate receptors (e.g. AMPA)

retrograde signalling hypothesis

a retrograde messenger

produced in postsynaptic cell

diffuses to presynaptic cell

increases the probability of neurotransmitter vesicle release

may be involved in induction of LTP (Pavlidis et al, 2000)

what's the difference between induction and expression??

unable to find a good candidate - considered nitric oxide and arachidonic acid - but since discarded (Malenka & Bear, 2004)

could be BDNF? or synaptic cell adhesion molecules (Sudhof, 2001)

gene transcription factors

synthesis of gene products is driven by kinases

these activate transcription factors that mediate gene expression

see CREB-1

cAMP response element binding protein-1 (CREB-1) = the primary transcription factor

necessary and sufficient for late LTP

active in its phosphorylated form

induces the transcription of immediate-early genes (c-fos and cojun)

gives rise to new building materials for the synapse

nitric oxide (maybe involved)

activate guanylyl cyclse

produce cGMP

activate protein kinase G (PKG)

phosphorylates CREB-1

release Ca++

possible structural remodelling

growth of new dendritic spines

enlargement of preexisting spines and associated postsynaptic densities (PSDs)

splitting of single PSDs and spines into two functional synapses (Abraham & Williams, 2003)

synaptic tagging

gene expression and protein synthesis take place in the cell body

but should be applied specifically to the synapses that have received LTP-inducing stimuli

syntactic tag = local protein synthesis that lasts less than three hours

sequesters plasticity-related proteins required to stabilize the early LTP

little is known about the identity of the synaptic tag or the proteins that are required to stabilize the early LTP
