Other animals

only the evolved primates can respond to objects that do not move

e.g. frogs cannot even see objects unless they are moving

they do not 'see' in the sense that we do - when their tongues flick out to catch flies, it is more like a reflex reaction to small, dark, fast-moving objects

Theories

Marr and Movshon proposed that information about motion in the visual field is extracted in two stages:

  • measure one-dimensional moving objects the motion of the components of complex objects
  • higher-order neurons combine and integrate the components of motion analysed by several of the initial stage neurons
