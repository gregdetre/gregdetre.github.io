nucleus reticularis pontis oralis

Characterized by

desynchronization of EEG

loss of skeletal muscle tone]] (atonia)

faster heartbeat and higher blood pressure than non-REM sleep (Legramante & Galante)

EEG voltage reduction]] (spindles and slow waves are blocked)

looks similar to stage 1

sometimes includes beta waves

Duration

20-25% of total sleep time

predominant in the last third of sleep

see:

sleep patterns

Regulated by

primarily by nucleus reticularis pontis oralis

EEG voltage reduction

EEG spindles and slow waves are blocked during REM

cholinergic neurons in midbrain and adjacent dorsal pons

maximally active during waking and REM

i think these must be the REM-waking-on cells

also release acetylcholine and other transmitters to depolarize the GABA-ergic inhibitory neurons in the reticularis nucleus

prevent activation of the low-threshold Ca2+ channels that initiate the rhythmic firing of the reticular neurons

so the thalamocortical relay cells fire asynchronously

Muscle tone

muscle tone is lost (atonia) during REM sleep because motor neurons are actively inhibited

put a cat on an island surrounded by water - falls into REM sleep and then falls off

damage to nucleus reticularis pontis oralis or to medial medulla can cause vigorous motor activity during REM sleep

abnormal activity in the nucleus reticularis pontis oralis REM-on cells can cause cataplexy, sleep apnea and REM sleep behavior disorder

Dreaming

if you wake someone up in REM sleep, they're more likely to report that they were dreaming

though it appears that dreaming can occur in non-REM, though less frequently and maybe more vividly during REM

no qualitative difference between REM and non-REM dreams of same length

longer dreams tend to be more emotional, vivid and less coherent)

and REM dreams tend to be longer

dream content is apparently not unduly influenced by external stimuli

Function

what happens if you don't get REM?

e.g. if you keep waking someone up when they fall into REM

most obvious consequence is that they get really irritable

then when you do let them sleep you get a REM rebound

just as underslept people have a general sleep rebound

lack of REM sleep may disrupt memory for tasks

but it's difficult to be sure that it's not just an effect of attention

if you sleep too little/much, just generally less healthy and more likely to die from every kind of disease

Other effects

penile erections and clitoral engorgement

pupils become highly constricted (miosis)

reflects high ratio of parasympathetic to sympathetic output to pupil

attenuated homeostatic mechanisms

easier to wake from REM than from stages 3 and 4
