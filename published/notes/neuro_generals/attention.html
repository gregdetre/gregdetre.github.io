Definition

"Everyone knows what attention is. It is the taking posession of the mind in clear and vivid form of one out of what seem several simultaneous objects or trains of thoughts"

  • William James

see also definition in cognitive control

Function

enhances ability to perceive and report visual attributes

or can render you blind to events

may guide voluntary eye movements

Mechanism

leftward shift in a neuron's contrast response function (Reynolds et ll, 2000)

response modulation found at all stages of processing, incl V1 (Motter, 1993)

however: stronger in more anterior extrastriate areas (Kastner et al, 1998), and also happens sooner

modulated by the anticipated difficulty of the discrimination

attention to (Corbetta et al, 1991):

shape, color -> greater activity in posterior portion of fusiform gyrus (incl V4)

speed -> MT/MST

Receptive fields

argue in object attention that there may be a computational bottleneck built into all levels of the visual system in the form of spatial receptive fields

multiple objects in the same RF can't all be transmitted at once

so you need to shrink the RFs around an object when it's being processed
