Controlled vs automatic processing

Definition

controlled vs automatic processing (Posner & Snyder, 1975)

controlled = those that rely on attention for execution

e.g. color naming in the stroop task

automatic processes = those that can be carried out without attention

e.g. word reading in the stroop task

Objections

not clear that

a cognitive process (e.g. word reading) can occur entirely independently of attention

color naming (controlled process) is always dependent on attention and control

MacLeod & Dunbar (1988)

learned associations between arbitrary shapes and arbitrary color-word-names

stroop task, naming shapes in conflicting colors

color naming started out seeming like the automatic process

after lots of training, the shape's name looked like the automatic process

i.e. interfered with color naming

all processes rely on attention to some degree (Kahneman & Treisman, 1984)

and this varies for a given process in different contexts

so they argue for a graded/relative spectrum of automatic/controlled
