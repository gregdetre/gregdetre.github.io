Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis

Aka

HPA axis

Part of

stress response

Consists of

hypothalamus

pituitary gland

adrenal gland

Inputs from

regulated by

hippocampus

central amygdaloid nucleus

Mechanism

during stressful situations

central amygdaloid nucleus regulates regulates periventricular hypothalamus

periventricular hypothalamus releases corticotropin releasing hormone (CRH)

anterior pituitary gland then releases adrenocorticotropic hormone in response into general circulation

ACTH then stimulates the release of cortisol from the adrenal gland

cortisol release -> physical stress response

eventually has an inhibitory effect on the periventricular hypothalamus, causing it to stop secreting CRH
