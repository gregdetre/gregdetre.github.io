Mechanism

fire when the monkey performs an action (e.g. grasping) and also when observing the same movement being performed by another monkey or even by a human

been observed in primates, some birds and humans

Rizzolatti, Fogassi and Gallese suggest that the mirror neurons form a basic system of action recognition

early stages of an internal model, since they are active both when the monkey performs and observes an action

originally found in macaques, which don't imitate each other

so it seems unlikely that they evolved for imitation learning

more likely to be involved in understanding what other monkeys are doing by recognizing their actions
