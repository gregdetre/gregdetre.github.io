Details

extensive bilateral auditory input in the CNS from the outset, so that lesions of the central auditory pathwya do not cause monaural disability

efferents from the superior olivary complex innervate hair cells, reducing their sensitivity, enabling them to respond to high sound levels

Medial nucleus of the superior olive

relatively large bodies, relatively large axons

innervate the outer hair cells

mainly on the contralateral side

the medial superior olive is concerned with sound localization on the basis of interaural time differences - this nucleus is composed of spindle-shaped neurons with one medial and one lateral dendrite, which receive input from the contralateral and ipsilateral cochlear nuclei, respectively. The binaural cells in the medial superior olive are very sensitive to phase differences between continuous tones presented to the two ears

Lateral group of the superior olive

smaller cell bodies, smaller axons

innervate the nerve fibers just below the inner hair cells

almost entirely on the ipsilateral side
