last updated Winter 2001

lots of philosophy-related things about rationality for my thesis:

Thomas Nagel,'The Last Word' - he's a frustratingly clever objectivist about reason

Stephen Stich, 'The fragmentation of reason'

Robert Nozick, 'The nature of rationality'

Steven Pinker, 'How the mind works'

Douglas Hofstadter, 'Godel, Escher, Bach' - deserves the praise it has garnered over the years

absolutely no fiction for what seems like a very long time

 

Simon Winchester, 'The Surgeon of Crowthorne' - traces the paths of two very different Victorians behind the OED

McLeod, Plunkett & Rolls, 'Introduction to connectionist modelling of cognitive processes' - well-written and helpful

Sams, 'C Unleashed' - considers a diverse mix of C-related things, including parallel computing, genetic algorithms, natural language processing and memory allocation

 

Greg Egan, 'Luminous' - short heavily-philosophical science fiction stories of staggering inventiveness and style

Mikhail Bulgakov, 'The Master and Margarita' - difficult to know what to say here, but definitely pretty amazing

Margaret Weis & Tracy Hickman, 'Dragonlance Legends vol I' - the best modern fantasy around

Stephen Levy, 'A-life' - surely this is the way forward???

David Gemmell, one of the indistinguishable Waylander books

Peter F Hamilton, 'The Night's Dawn trilogy' - awesome sci-fi saga
