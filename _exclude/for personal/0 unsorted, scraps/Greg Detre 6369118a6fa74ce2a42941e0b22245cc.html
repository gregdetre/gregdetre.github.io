Memrise LLM for language learning

Led development on the world’s first LLM-based language learning coach for Memrise, using the original GPT-3 API (in early 2022), multi-agent architecture, and custom human and automated evals/red-teaming.

Prompt engineering was difficult (before RLHF), so we ran AB tests with human evals to tune the results, plus automated evals (especially for protecting against malicious input). We also built a meta-prompt that generated other prompts, to produce a diverse range of content.

Memrise adaptive learning algorithms

Led design & development of algorithms for highly effective human learning, incorporating user-generated mnemonics, the spacing effect, test-driven learning, and adaptive difficulty.

Our algorithms worked for learning languages (from Japanese’s multiple alphabets, to Klingon), but also for everything from driving theory tests to birdsong. In informal testing, Memrise improved retention by an order of magnitude (compared with staring at a textbook), and was much more addictive.

The Multi-Voxel Pattern Analysis (MVPA) toolbox (2005-2010, scientific, open source)

In grad school, I led the development and maintenance of the popular Multi-Voxel Pattern Analysis Toolbox (in Matlab) for analysing 4D fMRI neuroimaging data with multivariate and machine learning methods (such as sparse linear classifiers).

We developed a number of state-of-the-art methods, and at the time our toolbox was the most popular software for applying machine learning to brain imaging analysis, downloaded by hundreds of labs around the world, and featured in multiple publications.

Emacs Freex mode (2005-2007, open source, personal)

An Emacs mode for massively-hyperlinked database of notes and ideas. Various novel features that predate Roam and Obsidian by 20 years, including full transclusion, a combined hierarchical/tagging representational system, and auto-hyperlinking. Still in active use, by exactly one person in the world.
